## 1. Grammar Spot

**Positive**: **There's** a sofa? **There are** two armchairs. <br>
**Questions**: **Is there** a TV? **Are there** any pictures? <br>
**Negative**: **There isn't** a bed. **There aren't** any books.

## 2. Grammar Spot 

**Positive**: There are **some** magazines. <br>
**Question**: Are there **any** magazines? <br>
**Negative**: There aren't **any** books.

## 4. Grammar reference

### 4.1 There is/There are

**Positive** <br>
**There's** a sofa in the living room. (There's = There is) <br>
**There are** two bedrooms in my house.

**Negative** <br>
**There isn't** a TV. <br>
**There aren't** any photos.

**Questions** <br>
**Is there** a TV in the kitchen? <br>
**Are there** any magazines on the table? <br>
How many lamps **are there**?

**Short answers**

|                                   |                                                |
|-----------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Is there a TV in the bedroom?     | Yes, **there is**. <br> No, **there isn't**.   |
| Are there any photos on the wall? | Yes, **there are**. <br> No, **there aren't**. |

### 4.2 Prepositions

[ **in** ] <br>
The Credit cards are **in** the drawer.


**on** <br>
⬚ <br>
There's lamp **on** the bedside table.

⬚ <br>
**under** <br>
The trainers are **under** the bed.

**next to** ⬚ <br>
There are some photos **next to** the books.

### 4.3 _some_ and _any_

We use _some_ in positive sentences. <br>
There are **some** books.

We use _any_ in questions and negatives. <br>
Does he have **any** photos? <br>
There aren't **any** lamps.
