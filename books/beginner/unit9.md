## 2. Grammar Spot

1. The negative of **was** is **wasn't** (= was not) <br>
The negative of **were** is **weren't** (= were not)
2. He **was** an architect. No, he **wasn't**. <br>
They **were** English. No, they **weren't**.

## 4. Grammar reference

### 4.1 was/were 
_Was_ and _were_ are the past tense of _am/are/is_.

**Present**

|                       |                                                  |
|-----------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| I                     | **am** <td rowspan="3">fine. <br> in class.</td> |
| He/She <br> It        | **is**                                           |
| You <br> We <br> They | **are**                                          |

**Past**

|                       |          |         |
|-----------------------|----------|---------|
| I <br> He/She <br> It | **was**  | fine.   |
| You <br> We <br> They | **were** | at home |

**Negative**

|               |                                                                                 | 
|---------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| I <br> He     | **wasn't** <td rawspan="2">at home last weekend. <br> at school yesterday.</td> |
| You <br> They | **weren't**                                                                     |

**Questions**

Where **were you** yesterday? <br>
**Was she** at school? <br>
Yes, **she was**./No, **she wasn't**.

> [!NOTE]
> We use _was/were_ with _born_, not _am/is/are_. <br>
> Where **were** you **born**? NOT ~~Where are you born?~~ <br>
> He **was born** in Russia. NOT ~~He is born in Russia.~~
