## 1. Grammar Spot

**Positive**: I **like** chocolate. <br>
**Negative**: I **don't like** tomatoes. **don't** = do not

## 2. Grammar Spot

**Positive**: I/You/We/They **like** chocolate. <br>
**Negative**: I/You/We/They **don't like** golf. <br>
**Question**: What **do** you/they **like**? **Do** you/they **like**...? <br>
**Short answers**: Yes, I/we/they **do**. **No**, I/we/they **don't**.

## 3. Grammar Spot

**Positive**: I/You/We/They **live** in a big house. <br>
**Negative**: I/You/We/They **don't play** baseball. <br>
**Questions**: **Do** you/they **speak** Polish? Where **do** you/they **come** from?

## 4. Grammar Spot
1. Adjectives (прилагательные) come before the noun. <br>
a **Japanese** car <br> 
Scottish whisky NOT ~~whisky Scottish~~
2. We don't add _-s_ to the adjective.
**Italian** shoes NOT Italian~~s~~ shoes

## 6. Grammar reference

### 6.1 Present Simple: I/you/we/they

**Positive**

|                                   |                                                                                                                         |
|-----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| I <br> You <br> We <br> They <br> | **like** coffee. <br> **play** tennis. <br> **live** in London. <br> **speak** two languages. <br> **have** a good job. |

**Negative**

|                                   |           |                                                                                                                         |
|-----------------------------------|-----------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| I <br> You <br> We <br> They <br> | **don't** | **like** coffee. <br> **play** tennis. <br> **live** in London. <br> **speak** two languages. <br> **have** a good job. |

**Questions with question words**

|                                   |                 |
|-----------------------------------|-----------------|
| Where <td rowspan="3">**do**</td> | you **live**?   |
| What sports                       | we **like**?    |
| How many languages                | they **speak**? |

**Yes/No** questions and short answers

|                                |                                           |
|--------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Do** you **like** football?  | Yes, I **do**. <br> No, I **don't**.      |
| **Do** they **speak** English? | Yes, they **do**. <br> No, they **don't** |

> [!NOTE]
> Do you like tea? Yes, I do. NOT ~~Yes, I like.~~

### 6.2 Adjective + noun (1)
Adjectives always come before the noun.

an **English** dictionary  ~~a dictionary English~~ <br>
a **Japanese** car ~~a car Japanese~~ <br>
a **beautiful** girl ~~a girl beautiful~~ <br>
**Italian** shoes ~~Italians shoes~~
