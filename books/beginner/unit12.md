## 1. Grammar Spot
**_would like/would like to_** <br>
1. _I'd like_ - is more polite than **_I want_** <br>
**I'd like** some ham, please.
2. We offer things with **_Would you like...?_** and **_Would you like to...?_**
**Would you like** anything else? <br>
**Would you like to watch a film?**

**_some_** and **_any_**
1. We use **_some_** in positive sentences. <br>
I'd like **some** apples.
2. We use **_any_** with questions and negatives. <br>
Do you have **any** oranges? <br>
We don't have **any** oranges.

We can also use **_some_** when we offer things. <br>
Would you like **some** coffee?

## 2. Grammar Spot
**Difference between _like_ and _'d like_**
1. **_Like_** refers to **always** 
I **like** coffee. I **like** going to the cinema.
2. **_'d like_** refers to **now** or **soon**
**I'd like** a coffee, please. **I'd like to** go to the cinema tonight.

## 3. Grammar reference
### 3.1 would like

1. We use **_would like_** to ask for things.
**Positive**

|                                              |             |               |
|----------------------------------------------|-------------|---------------|
| I <br/> You <br/> He/She <br/> We <br/> They | **'d like** | a cup of tea. |

2. We use **_Would ... like?_** to offer things.
**Question**

|           |                             |          |            |
|-----------|-----------------------------|----------|------------|
| **Would** | you <br/> he/she <br/> they | **like** | some cake? |

3. Answers

**_Would you like_** a cup of tea? Yes, please./No, thank you.

We use **_would like_**, not **_want_** to be polite. <br>
**_I'd like_** a coffee, please. NOT ~~I want a coffee.~~

4. We can use **_would like_** with another verb.

**Would** you **like to go out** tonight? <br>
What **would** you **like to do?**

### 4.3 _like_ and _would like_

1. We use **_like_** and **_like doing_** to talk about things we always like.
I **like** coffee. (= I always enjoy coffee.) <br>
She **likes** swimming in summer. (= She always enjoys swimming.)
2. We use **_would like_** to talk about things we want now or soon.
I<b>'d like</b> a coffee. (= I want a coffee.) <br>
I<b>'d like</b> to go shopping. (= I want to go shopping.)
