## 1. Grammar Spot
1. **The Present Continuous** describes what is happening **_now_**. <br>
   He<b>'s wearing</b> a grey suit. <br>
   They<b>'re eating</b> ice cream. <br>
   I<b>'m sitting</b> in the classroom.
2. We make the **Present Continuous** with the verb **to be: am/is/are** + verb + **_-ing_**. <br>
   I<b>'m studying</b> English. <br>
   You/We/They<b>'re wearing</b> jeans. <br>
   She/He<b>'s playing</b> in the garden.

## 2. Grammar Spot
He **wears** a suit for work - Present Simple (is about every day).
He<b>'s wearing</b> a T-shirt. - Present Continuous (is about now).

### 3.2 Present Simple and Present Continuous
1. We use the Present Simple to talk about actions that are true for all time or a long time. <br>
   Hans **comes** from Germany. <br>
   I **love** you. <br>
   Му father **works** in а bank. <br>
   I **get up** at 7.30 every day. <br>
   She **doesn't** understand French.
2. We use the Present Continuous to talk about actions that last a short time. The actions are happening now. <br>
   I usually wear jeans, but today I<b>'m wearing</b> а suit. <br>
   Не speaks French very well. He<b>'s speaking</b> French to that man. <br>
   It<b>'s raining</b>. <br>
   They<b>'re swimming</b>. <br>
   We<b>'re playing</b> football.
