## 2. Grammar Spot

**Adverbs:**
always - 100% <br>
usually - 75% <br>
sometimes - 25% <br>
never - 0%

## 3. Grammar Spot

1. **Positive** <br>
She **gets up** at 6.00. <br>
She **has** breakfast at 6.30 <br>
2. **Negative** <br>
She **doesn't have** lunch at home. **doesn't** = does not <br>
She **doesn't go** to work by car. 
3. **Questions** <br>
What time **does** she **get up**? <br>
**Does** she **work** late? Yes, she **does**./No, she **doesn't**.

## 4. Grammar reference

### 4.1 Present Simple: he/she/it (1)

**Positive**

|             |                                             |
|-------------|---------------------------------------------|
| He <br> She | get<b>s</b> up <td rowspan="2">at 8.00</td> |
| It          | leave<b>s</b>                               |

### 4.2 Spelling - Present Simple: he/she/it

1. Most verbs add _-s_

|           |                                                     |
|-----------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| He/She/It | listen<b>s</b> <br> leave<b>s</b> <br> walk<b>s</b> |

2. Verbs ending in _-s_, _-ss_', _-sh_, _-ch_ add _-es_

|           |                                   |
|-----------|-----------------------------------|
| He/She/It | watch<b>es</b> <br> wash<b>es</b> |

> [!NOTE]
> _go, have_ and _do_ are irregular

|           |                                     |
|-----------|-------------------------------------|
| He/She/It | **does** <br> **goes** <br> **has** |

### 4.3 Adverbs of frequency

0% - never
25% - sometimes
75% - usually
100% - always

Adverbs of frequency (_never, sometimes, usually, always_) can come before the verb.

We **never** drink beer. <br>
She **sometimes** goes out on a Saturday night. <br>
He **usually** works late. <br>
I **always** have tea for breakfast.

### 4.4 Present Simple: he/she/it (2)

**Negative**

|             |             |                                                         |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------------------------------------------|
| He <br> She | **doesn't** | **go out** in the evening. <br> **eat** in a restaurant |

**Questions with question words**

|                                    |                        |
|------------------------------------|------------------------|
| What <td rowspan="4">**does**</td> | he **have** for lunch? |
| Where                              | she **work**?          |
| What time                          | he **go** to bed?      |
| When                               | he **leave** work?     |

**Yes/No** questions and short answers

|                                 |                                             |
|---------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| **Does** he **like** football?  | Yes, he **does**. <br> No, he **doesn't**.  |
| **Does** she **speak** English? | Yes, she **does**. <br> No, she **doesn't** |

> [!NOTE]
> Does he like tea? Yes, he does. NOT ~~Yes, he likes.~~ <br>
> Do you like coffee? No, I don't. NOT ~~No, I don't like.~~
