## 1. Grammar Spot
Here are two ways of talking about future plans.
1. **Present Continuous** <br>
They<b>'re leaving</b> on Saturday at six o'clock. <br>
We<b>'re flying</b> to New York tomorrow/next week.
2. **going to** <br>
They<b>'re going to have</b> a boat cruise on the River Tagus. <br>
We<b>'re going to walk</b> round old port.

## 2. Grammar reference

### 2.1 Future plans

#### Positive
**Present Continuous**

|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| I<b>'m</b> <br/> You<b>'re</b> <br/> He<b>'s</b> <br/> She<b>'s</b> <br/> We<b>'re</b> <br/> They<b>'re</b> | **going** to Europe. <br/> **leaving** next week. <br/> **flying on Sunday**. |

**Going to**

|                                                                                                             |              |                                                                                                |
|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
